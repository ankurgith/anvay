About Me

Assistant Professor at Plaksha University | Ph.D. from IIT Jodhpur

I am an academic and engineer whose work bridges the gap between theoretical network protocols and real-world deployment. I earned my Ph.D. under the mentorship of Dr. Debasis Das (IIT Jodhpur) and Prof. Sajal K. Das (Missouri S&T).

My work focuses on self-healing programmable networks, resource allocation, and congestion avoidance. I have also served in leadership roles, including Secretary of the ACM Student Chapter.

Notable Projects as a Student

Cognitive Radio Based Technology for Smart City Applications (India-Uzbekistan Project)

Overview: The project is a part of a collaborative initiative between researchers and professionals from India and Uzbekistan on "Cognitive Radio Based Technology for Smart City Applications". The project vision is to harness the power of cognitive radio technology to address the evolving challenges faced by modern cities in India and Uzbekistan. The major goal of this project is to build a network architecture with cognitive management framework for a scalable collaborative network and addresses the central issue of bandwidth allocation and rural outreach.

Secure Communication and Searching System for Vehicular Cloud Computing (India-Australia Research Project)

Overview: The primary focus of this project is to develop a robust and efficient edge computing framework that facilitates secure communication between vehicles within the Internet of Vehicles (IoV). This project is aimed at addressing these needs by delivering fast responses to various infotainment and entertainment services in a secure manner.
